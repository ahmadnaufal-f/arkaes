01 · Brand Foundation

A frontend engineer with a deep respect for systems and an obsession with the visual. Every interface decision is deliberate — beauty and function are never separate concerns.

Blush

Primary hover states, eyebrow lines, italic title color, scroll indicators, CTA underlines, stat numbers, and marquee dividers.

Sage

Maximum 5 placements per page. Reserved for: portrait bottom rule, service row hover border, hero composition block, social links in dark sections, and type scale accent.

Ink

All body text, headlines, and primary UI. Never replace with a brand color unless semantically intentional. The ink does the heavy lifting.

Warm White

Page background only. Never use pure white (#ffffff) — always use Warm White (#faf7f5). The warmth is the difference between sterile and refined.

04 · Logo & Wordmark

The Wordmark

The Æ ligature is the defining mark of the brand. It is the junction of ARK and ES — the visual shorthand for "architecture meets aesthetics."

ARKÆS
Primary · Light Background
ARKÆS
Reversed · Dark Background
ARKÆS
Mono / Text-only variant — for environments that don't support Fraunces
Typeface

Fraunces — weight 200 (thin). Never bold. Never condensed. The delicacy of the weight is intentional.

The Æ

The ae ligature is the defining mark. Always Blush Deep (#b8887a) on light, Blush Light (#e8c8bc) on dark. Always italic. Never replaced with "AE".

Letter-spacing

0.08em tracking on the full wordmark. Enough breathing room, not enough to fragment.

Minimum size

80px on digital. The Æ legibility must be preserved at all sizes. Below 80px, fall back to the mono variant.

Clear space

Minimum clear space = cap height of the A on all four sides. Never crowd the wordmark.

Do
  • Use the Æ ligature — never "AE" or "Ae"
  • Maintain Fraunces weight 200 always
  • Use Blush Deep for Æ on light backgrounds
  • Respect minimum clear space on all sides
  • Use the mono variant when Fraunces is unavailable
Don't
  • Never stretch or condense the wordmark
  • Never apply the full wordmark in a single brand color
  • Never add drop shadows, gradients, or glow effects
  • Never recreate in a different typeface
  • Never place on a busy background without sufficient contrast
05 · Voice & Tone

How Arkaes Speaks

Calm authority. Never loud. Never generic. Confident without being boastful. The work leads — the words follow.

01
Precise

Exact language. No filler phrases. Every word earns its place. If it can be removed without loss, remove it.

02
Warm

Approachable craft — not cold expertise. The work invites, not intimidates. People should want to work with Arkaes.

03
Considered

Thoughtful explanations of why, not just what. Process is visible. Decisions are explained, not just displayed.

04
Understated

The work speaks first. Self-description is minimal and factual. No superlatives, no passion-for-excellence clichés.

05
Curious

Genuine interest in problems. Questions are as valued as answers. Arkaes is always learning — and makes that visible.

Project description · Do

"A design system built for a multinational engineering team — 40+ components, zero visual inconsistencies, shipped in 6 weeks."

Project description · Don't

"I am a passionate and driven developer with a love for creating amazing user experiences that delight users and stakeholders."

About copy · Do

"Frontend engineer. Obsessed with the intersection of systems and craft."

About copy · Don't

"I'm a creative individual who loves bringing ideas to life through the power of technology and innovative design thinking!"

Call to action · Do

"Let's build something remarkable." — Never "Let's connect!" or "Hire me!"

07 · Usage Rules

Brand Integrity

What protects the brand — and what breaks it. These rules exist because the brand's refinement is fragile. One wrong choice dilutes years of consistency.

Do
  • Use exact palette values — no approximations or eyeballed matches.
  • Maintain Fraunces weight 200 for all display type.
  • Apply grain texture at 2.0–2.5% opacity only.
  • Keep sage usage to a maximum of 5 placements per page.
  • Use Warm White (#faf7f5) — never pure white (#ffffff).
  • Ensure every project shown reflects the Arkaes aesthetic.
  • Use the Æ ligature in the wordmark — always.
  • Maintain 80px minimum wordmark size on digital.
Don't
  • Never apply the full brand in dark mode — light mode only for arkaes.dev.
  • Never use more than 3 typefaces together on one layout.
  • Never use blush and sage at equal visual weight on the same page.
  • Never apply drop shadows, gradients, or glow effects on type.
  • Never show work that contradicts the refined aesthetic.
  • Never animate more than 2 elements simultaneously.
  • Never use exclamation marks in brand copy.
  • Never modify the Æ color outside the brand specification.
